# AEGIS — Five-slide presentation

## Slide 1 — The problem

### Title

Emergency teams do not lack information. They lack a trusted operating picture.

### Content

- Resident reports, weather, closures, shelters, and field updates arrive through disconnected channels.
- Reports can be duplicated, contradictory, inaccessible, or wrong.
- Delayed verification slows response; automated decisions can amplify bias.

### Speaker point

During a flood, the dangerous gap is between receiving a signal and coordinating a verified action.

## Slide 2 — The people and the product

### Title

AEGIS turns fragmented signals into human-authorized action.

### Stakeholders

- Residents and people requiring accessibility support
- Emergency operations coordinators
- Fire, paramedic, transit, and shelter teams
- Public communications officers
- Municipal accountability and privacy teams

### Product promise

One incident moves through detection, verification, resource coordination, public communication, and audit.

## Slide 3 — The agentic workflow and live demonstration

### Title

Seven agents, two mandatory human decisions.

### Workflow

1. Signal agent groups relevant reports.
2. Verification agent finds corroboration and conflict.
3. Situation agent extracts hazards and needs.
4. Priority agent applies explicit escalation rules.
5. Response agent proposes routes and resources.
6. Communications agent drafts bilingual instructions.
7. Accountability agent records evidence and decisions.

### Human gates

- Verify the incident.
- Approve response assignments and authorize the public warning.

### Demonstration

Run the complete workflow from Live Operations to Accountability.

## Slide 4 — Responsible AI by design

### Title

The system is designed to preserve uncertainty and human authority.

### Controls

- Classification never equals verification.
- Confidence and severity are separate.
- Unverified claims remain visibly unverified.
- Human overrides require a reason.
- Low-reporting areas are not assumed safe.
- Accessibility resources are included in response planning.
- Sensitive identifiers are removed from historical report examples.
- Every AI recommendation and human authorization is auditable.

## Slide 5 — Evidence and value

### Title

Built on public evidence; designed for operational resilience.

### Data

- 169,109 labelled historical disaster reports
- 58 Toronto climate variables across observed and future periods
- 1,023 TransformTO resident survey responses
- Five Montréal emissions sectors
- Climate indicators for 54 African countries

### Operational services

- Open-Meteo weather
- OpenStreetMap mapping
- OSRM routing
- Gemini 3.5 Flash structured incident analysis
- Deterministic fallbacks for every external dependency

### Closing line

AEGIS does not replace emergency judgment. It gives that judgment a faster, clearer, and accountable operating picture.
